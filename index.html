A GUIDE TO PROCRASTINATE CREATIVELY

You are here to be guided through the process of creative thinking

Use this procrastination time as a space to brew your creativity and marinade your ideas

Let's procrastinate and get inspired together!

This guide is inspired by the thinking models proposed by Graham Wallas and James Webb Young.

It is designed to guide you through the 4 stages of creative process, helping you to kickstart your creative projects

LET'S START OUR CREATIVE JOURNEY NOW!

STAGE 2:
INCUBATION

Now put everything to the back of your mind
Forget about what you saw and thought
Engage yourself with other tasks and start procrastinating
Let the ideas naturally come to you

If you can't think of anything to do, try this procrastination generator ↓


STAGE 3:
ILLUMINATION

During Incubation, connections between researches were made subconciously in your mind. Illumination gives time for those connections to resurface and appear conciously

Use the following creative exercises as triggers to help ideas form

1. Do a blind portrait of your friend. Try not to look at the canvas while you draw
2. Construct a narrative with the three randomly generated words below

3. Finish the incomplete drawing

STAGE 4:
VERIFICATION

With connections being built, you now should have some ideas which you want to try working on. It's time for you to organise those ideas and plan for experimentations to test their validity and practicality.

Talk to someone, be engaged in coversation to get feedbacks for improvements.
